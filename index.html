Dr. Jamal Giri is an Associate Professor in the Department of Orthodontics at B.P. Koirala Institute of Health Sciences (BPKIHS), Dharan, Nepal — one of the premier medical institutions in South Asia. He completed his PhD at the Adelaide Dental School, University of Adelaide, Australia in October 2025, where his doctoral research focused on the genetic and environmental contributions to dental arch development and facial morphology using a longitudinal twin study design.

With over a decade of teaching and clinical experience, Dr. Giri has taught hundreds of undergraduate and postgraduate dental students, supervised research projects, and provided specialised orthodontic care — including for patients with cleft lip and palate. Since December 2025, he also serves as Assistant Dean Research at BPKIHS, where he facilitates research activities and strengthens international collaborations.

His scholarly output spans 35+ peer-reviewed publications in prestigious journals including the American Journal of Orthodontics and Dentofacial Orthopedics and the European Journal of Orthodontics, alongside numerous international conference presentations. He holds additional postgraduate certificates in Clinical Education (Edinburgh) and Medical Education (Nottingham).
